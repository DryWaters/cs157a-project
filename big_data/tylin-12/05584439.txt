Abstract The aim of this work is to present a particular design technique of hierarchical fuzzy controllers. The method makes an easy way to control complex systems with an automatic low complicated design It was based on the systems capability to generate output informations when exited outside any control instance. This fact helps constructing input-output learning and testing data. This type of fuzzy controller is made optimal by the use of hierarchical minimal association of input rule bases, learned fuzzy inferences and the configuration of aggregation processes. The proposed strategy of design with learned fuzzy rule bases guarantee an interpretable behavioral decomposition.
